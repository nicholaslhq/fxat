How to read the Dual-Axis Chart

  • Comparing Performance: The dotted line is the stock price in its original currency. Compare it with the solid line to see if the exchange rate is helping or hurting.
  • When lines cross: If the solid line is above the dotted line, the Target Currency has weakened against the Base. This makes your stock more valuable when converted back.
  • Market vs. Currency: If the dotted line goes up but the solid line stays flat, your stock gains were cancelled out because the Target Currency strengthened.
  • Understanding the Gap: A small gap means the currency is stable. A large gap means significant currency movement is boosting or dragging your final value.
